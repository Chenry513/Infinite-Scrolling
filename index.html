Interventions for well-being based on Irmer & Schmiedek (2023) and Yan et al. (2024)

Paper 1
Upward Comparison & Well-being
Irmer & Schmiedek (2023)
Social media triggers upward comparisons (feeling others are better off), which negatively impacts self-worth and well-being in youth.
Solutions in Demo
Reality check - shows creator's resources/team size
Paid/Edited badges - flags sponsored & produced content
Mood check-in - asks how user is feeling after similar content
Regular creator badge - highlights non-influencer content

Paper 2
Attention & Self-Control
Yan et al. (2024)
Short video addiction is linked to reduced executive control and lower self-control, measured via EEG frontal theta activity.
Solutions in Demo
Ad breaks - forced pause every 30 videos to interrupt dopamine loop
Desaturation - colors gradually fade over extended use
Longer videos - algorithm shifts to 2-3 min content to break rapid consumption
